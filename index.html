Welcome to Full Harvest Hub!

Full Harvest operates as a B2B marketplace utilizing technology to forge direct connections between food and beverage companies and farms. It streamlines the procurement process for all types of produce, including surplus and imperfect ones, with the goal of minimizing food waste and achieving complete harvests. By adopting this innovative model, Full Harvest contributes to building a more sustainable and effective produce supply chain.

The Full Harvest Hub addresses the significant issue of agricultural waste stemming from surplus production. Despite advancements in farming technology, inefficiencies in distribution and consumption persist, resulting in vast quantities of perfectly edible produce going to waste. This surplus not only represents a lost opportunity for feeding communities but also exerts significant environmental strain through increased greenhouse gas emissions and wasted resources. On our website, Full Harvest Hub, we prominently display images depicting this waste, serving as a stark reminder of the urgent need for change. Through platforms like ours, we strive to address this issue head-on by providing solutions that optimize the utilization of all harvested crops, fostering a more sustainable and equitable food system for all.

Why Full Harvest is needed?

Food Waste

Full Harvest reduces food waste by facilitating the purchase of surplus and imperfect produce, ensuring that all harvested crops are utilized effectively.
Sustainability

By optimizing the use of available produce, Full Harvest contributes to a more sustainable agricultural ecosystem, reducing the environmental impact of wasted resources and greenhouse gas emissions.
Improve Supply Chain Efficiency

The Full Harvest method streamlines the produce procurement process, connecting food and beverage companies directly with farms, thus enhancing the efficiency of the agricultural supply chain.
Increase Access to Fresh Produce

Through Full Harvest, food and beverage companies gain access to a wider range of produce, including surplus and imperfect items, which may otherwise go unsold or be discarded, thus increasing overall accessibility to fresh produce.
Economic Viability

Full Harvest helps farms maximize their profits by selling all grades of produce, including surplus and imperfect ones. This economic viability promotes the long-term sustainability of farms and rural communities.
